Eye-Tap

EyeTap is the world's first digital eyeglass, invented by Professor Mann in 1984, almost 30 years before Google Glass arrived in 2013. EyeTap displays computer information to the user and allows the computer to process and augment what the user sees.

There are endless potential applications for EyeTap for both personal and commercial use. One use, for instance, would be a sports EyeTap.

We are all Makers

Following the MIT models, at MannLab everyone engages in "jam sessions" (akin to playing jazz music) where we collaborate on ideas that motivate us, bringing about the greatest successes. Employeeing "demo-or-die", projects move through the lab quickly, receiving the full dedication of our forces each and every day to produce a new visible result.
